# Goals — Stress and Energy Module

## Mastery target (extended encoding, §4)

> Given an unfamiliar health claim about stress and energy (e.g. "chronic stress lowers thyroid output"), trace the full causal chain: what system initiates the response, what hormones are involved, what downstream effects are plausible, and what evidence would be needed to evaluate the claim.

This is the schema-building peak. The learner should be able to do this without prompting for any well-formed claim in this space.

## Minimum bar to advance from the module (§9)

The learner can:

1. Explain in plain language what ATP is and why cells need it
2. Name the two main components of the stress response (HPA axis and sympathetic nervous system) and the primary hormone each produces
3. Explain allostatic load in their own words — what it is, why it accumulates, and why it matters
4. Distinguish between acute stress (adaptive) and chronic stress (damaging) and explain why the same response has different outcomes
5. Identify at least two downstream systems that chronic cortisol elevation affects

## Ideal bar (real mastery, §9)

The learner can additionally:

1. Explain why the "borrowed energy" framing in popular health writing is partially right and partially misleading
2. Look at a claim from a health article and identify which parts are well-supported by the stress biology framework and which parts require additional evidence
3. Name the key researchers (Sapolsky, McEwen) and what each contributed, well enough to find primary sources independently

## Non-goals

- Memorizing hormone names, gland locations, or clinical thresholds — surface recall without schema is not the goal
- Dietary or supplementation conclusions — this module stops at mechanism; application is a separate question
- Clinical diagnosis — this is research literacy, not medical knowledge
