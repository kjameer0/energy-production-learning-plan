## What this node is

The specific effects of chronically elevated cortisol on three systems the article focuses on: sleep, digestion, and thyroid function. This node feeds into allostatic load by grounding it in mechanism — allostatic load is not abstract, it is cortisol doing concrete things to concrete systems over time.

This node is deliberately scoped. The full list of cortisol's downstream effects is long; this module covers only the three most relevant to the article's claims and to your stated goal of understanding stress and energy.

## Sources

- **Sleep:** Sapolsky, *Why Zebras Don't Get Ulcers*, Chapter 11 — "Stress and Sleep." Covers how cortisol disrupts sleep architecture and why disrupted sleep elevates cortisol (the bidirectional loop).
  Estimated time: 25–35 minutes.

- **Digestion:** Sapolsky, *Why Zebras Don't Get Ulcers*, Chapter 5 — "Ulcers, Irritable Bowel Syndrome, and the Danger of Feeling Good." How the stress response shuts down digestion in the short term and what chronic inhibition does to the gut.
  Estimated time: 25–35 minutes.

- **Thyroid:** The HPA-thyroid axis interaction is less well-covered in Sapolsky. Use this PubMed review as the source:
  Tsigos C, Chrousos GP (2002). "Hypothalamic-pituitary-adrenal axis, neuroendocrine factors and stress." *Journal of Psychosomatic Research*, 53(4), 865–871.
  Search PubMed: Tsigos Chrousos 2002 HPA axis stress. Read the sections on thyroid. This is a denser paper — skim for the mechanism, not the clinical data.

## Schema-building exercises

1. **The sleep loop.** Cortisol disrupts sleep, and poor sleep elevates cortisol. Draw or describe this feedback loop. What would break the cycle in each direction? Why does this make sleep the most important variable to fix before addressing anything else?

2. **Digestion under stress.** The stress response shuts down digestion because digestion is expensive and non-urgent during a threat. Explain what "shuts down" means mechanically — what specifically stops or slows? Then explain why this matters for energy if the shutdown becomes chronic.

3. **Thyroid and cortisol.** The article claims cortisol suppresses thyroid function. Based on the Tsigos & Chrousos paper, is this accurate? What is the mechanism, and how strong is the effect? This is a place to be precise rather than accepting the article's framing wholesale.

4. **Hierarchy of effects.** If you had to rank sleep, digestion, and thyroid function by how much cortisol affects them and how quickly, what would that order be? Justify it from the sources.
